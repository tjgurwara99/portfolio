Accessibility
We want everyone to feel welcome at the ball and so we are working on a number of measures to ensure the ball is as accessible as possible:

• There will be a number of ‘chill-out areas’ with less noise and fewer people.
• There will be an accessible toilet suitable for the number of guests expected.
• We are also arranging an accessible entrance to the ball but since the site is still being finalised, we do not yet have the full information for this.
If you need more information relating to accessibility, please contact Charlotte Farmer (ball.vp@lmh.ox.ac.uk)

Sustainability
Sustainability is an important goal of ours, so we are working with the SustainaBall campaign to endeavour to minimise the ball’s impact on the environment. We are also working closely with our caterers to ensure the ingredients used are sourced as locally as possible and packaged using biodegradable materials. Some other measures we have in place include using a carbon-neutral production company, using hired and recycled decorations and having clearly marked recycling bins.

What’s a Pound?
We are honoured to announce that we will be working with "What's A Pound?" to raise money for The School Bus Project!

“The School Bus Project is a mobile refugee education project, working from the foundation principle that 'every child has the right to an education' (Article 26, UN Convention of Human Rights). Established and run by educational specialists, we work to provide stimulating, trauma-aware educational opportunities for children and young people who would not otherwise have the opportunity to attend school of any kind, due to their experiences as refugees and migrants. As a registered charity, we are wholly dependent on donations from individuals, organisations and grant-based funding. Heartfelt thanks to LMH for your support.”
